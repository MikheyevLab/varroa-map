Varroa destructor world collection

This world map shows the ongoing progess of the global sampling of honey bee ectoparasites Varroa. The collection aims to cover as many regions as possible in order to study the biogeography and demographic history of Varroa destructor using whole genome sequencing.

We are extremely grateful to all contributors to this project. This ambitious work would not be possible without your precious help!

Sampling collection on honey bees (mainly A. mellifera but also A. cerana) with the following idea:

Per colony: 2 to 30 mites (or more) are collected alive from adults or brood then freshly killed in one tube containing <90% ethanol.

Per site/apiary: flexible but ideally 5 to 15 colonies. One tube per colony.

Per country/region: also flexible, 3 to 5 distant sites or different beekeepers.

If you are interested in participating please contact Sasha Mikheyev or Maeva Techer at the Ecology and Evolution lab, Okinawa Institute of Sciences and Technology

Important: Japan importation rules are strict and only dead and preserved mites are seek for this project as importation of honey bees is completely forbidden (whole or parts).

Map raster shows the spread of V. destructor according to history records modified from Wilfert et al. 2016: recent (yellow) to old (red).
